Short Summary
Logarithms are essentially expressions that state that what known number raised to an unknown number will equate to a certain value. Let us begin with a simple logarithmic expression: $$\color{#04afff}{\log_515 = y}$$ If the subscript of the log, or 5, which is also known as the base, is not present, then the default value of it will be 10. This log expression states that 5 raised to the value of y will equate to 15, the inner value or the argument. $$\color{#04afff}{5^y=15}$$ Since the base will always be raised to a certain power to find the argument, or 15 in this circumstance, the argument cannot be negative, as any positive, non-zero value raised to any number will always be positive.
