Short Summary
There are a plethora of significant log properties that are necessary to solve and simplify some expressions, and the proof behind them are based on how exponent rules function. But, it is sufficient enough to simply memorize these logarithmic properties, as their proofs are not necessary until higher levels of mathematics, where proofs are necessary and useful. $$\color{#04afff}{\log_b1 = 0\\\\\log_b(xy) = \log_bx+\log_by\\\\\log_b(\frac{x}{y})= \log_bx - \log_by \\\\ \log_b(x^y) = y\log_b(x)\\\\ \log_bx = \frac{\log_{c}x}{\log_{c}b} \\\\ \log_b(b^x)=x}$$
