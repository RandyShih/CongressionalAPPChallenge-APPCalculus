Short Summary
Factoring is the act of essentially simplifying an equation to a form that allows people to easily find the zeros, or x-intercepts, of a function or be able to simplify a function through cancelling out certain terms. We will get to the purpose of factoring later on, but let us begin with how to factor. So, assume that we have an equation as follows: $$\color{#04afff}{x^2+4x+3}$$In order for this method of factoring, known as the diamond method to work, the equation must be in the form of a trinomial, or a polynomial with three terms. Now to begin using the diamond method, first identify the coefficients of the trinomial, and an easy way to do this would be to just find the constants of the equation with this framework of a trinomial: $$\color{#04afff}{ax^2 + bx + c}$$ As we in see here, in reference to our assumed equation, the value of a will be 1, as even though there is nothing in front of the first term, there will always be a value of 1 present. Now, onto the two other terms, which we can see the value of b being 4 and c being 3. Now that we have these values, draw an X and put the value of a * c onto the top of the X and the value of b in the bottom of the X. Now, you see that the values on the right and left of the X is blank, and this will be the values that you will be finding that abides by these equations: $$\color{#04afff}{t + h = b \\ t * h = a * c}$$ Essentially, the two values that go into those sides of the X will have to both have to sum up to the value of b and produce the value of a * c. Once you have found your values, I would recommend you to start finding what multiplied together equates to a * c, get the first term of your original equation, add it by both of your found values multiplied by x, and add the value of c to the equaiton (ignore the value of b). $$\color{#04afff}{x^2 + 3x + 1x + 3}$$ The order of the two middle terms does not matter, and now, separate them into their own factor (reference the vocabulary page). $$\color{#04afff}{(x^2+3x)(1x+3)}$$ Now, take out, or factor out, the least common denominator of each factor. $$\color{#04afff}{x(x+3)1(x+3)}$$ Reminder that you have to still take out 1 if a least common denominator is not present, and now that you have this equation, we can see that the two terms, after being factored out, is the same, which is not a coincidence and implies that you have done it right. Now, add the two factored out values together into one faactor and take one of the terms from the equation and multiply it together by the factor, as seen below. $$\color{#04afff}{Factored\, form: \,(x+1)(x+3)}$$ Now, there are some situations in which the value of a does not equate to 1, so just remember to always multiply c by a instead of immediately using the value of c and simply use the diamond as normal to solve those types of equations.
