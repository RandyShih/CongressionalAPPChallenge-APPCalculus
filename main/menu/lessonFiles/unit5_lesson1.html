Short Summary
One basic part of trigonometry is knowing how to conver radians to degrees and vice versa as trigonometric function are able to accept both degree and radians. Radians typically have the value of Pi multiplied to a coefficient, but they can also be any value, while degrees, which are usually between 0-360, can also exceed that limitation or become negative. Radians and degrees represent the amount of movement around a circle, with the movement going counterclockwise. If the radian or degree value exceeds the circle's allowed rotation, like walking past your starting point as you walk in a circle, then you will be looping through the same values of a trigonometric function. Although, this concept only applies to trigonometric functions like cos(x) and sin(x). The formula for converting from radian to degree and vice versa is as follows: $$\color{#04afff}{degrees = \frac{180°}{\pi} * radians\\\\ radians = \frac{\pi}{180°}*degrees}$$
